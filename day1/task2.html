Animals are living beings found all over the world. They can be divided into different types based on their characteristics. Wild animals like lions, tigers, and elephants live in forests. Domestic animals like cows, dogs, and cats live with humans. Pet animals such as rabbits, parrots, and goldfish are kept for companionship. Herbivores eat only plants, while carnivores eat other animals. Some animals are omnivores, eating both plants and meat, like bears and humans. Animals are also grouped as birds, fish, reptiles, amphibians, and insects. Each type of animal plays an important role in nature. Together, all animals help to maintain the balance of our environment.

The tiger is a wild animal and the national animal of India. It is a strong and powerful creature, known as the king of the jungle. Tigers have a yellowish-orange body with black stripes. They are very good hunters and can run fast to catch their prey. Tigers usually eat deer, wild boar, and other animals. They live in forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps. A tiger is also an excellent swimmer and enjoys water. Sadly, tigers are endangered because of hunting and loss of forests. Many wildlife sanctuaries and national parks protect them. The tiger is a symbol of bravery, strength, and pride.
